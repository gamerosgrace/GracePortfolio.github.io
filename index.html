FIG. 01 — ABOUT

I'm an Industrial Design Engineer specializing in packaging development, structural design, graphic packaging, and product innovation.

FIG. 02 — DESIGN PROCESS
01 / Discover

Discover

Understand the brand, the product, and the shelf it needs to win on — audience, constraints, and goals defined up front.

02 / Research

Research

Category audits, material research, and manufacturing feasibility studies inform every decision that follows.

03 / Concept

Concept Development

Multiple structural and visual directions explored in parallel, evaluated against cost, shelf impact, and brand fit.

04 / Structure

Structural Design

Dielines, load testing, and material specification — engineering the box to protect, ship, and display.

05 / Graphics

Graphic Design

Brand identity, color, and typography applied to the structure — designed to perform at arm's length and at a glance.

06 / 3D

3D Visualization

Photoreal renders validate proportion, print registration, and shelf presence before a single prototype is cut.

07 / Prototype

Prototype

Physical samples built and hand-assembled to confirm fit, function, and finish.

08 / Test

Testing

Drop, compression, and transit testing validate the structure holds up from pallet to shelf.

09 / Production

Production

Supplier qualification, press checks, and pre-production management ensure the design translates at scale.

10 / Final

Final Product

A manufacturable, shelf-ready product — engineered, validated, and ready to ship.

Challenge

Design a generic tomato shipping box that competes for attention in a crowded produce aisle while surviving the rigors of harvest, transit, and retail handling.

Objectives

Convey freshness, quality, and regional origin at a glance; keep the structure efficient to produce and strong enough for stacked transit.

Solution

Objectives

Warm, appetite-driving color and a hand-lettered mark to stand apart from clinical, corporate produce branding; retain CAT 1 grading and net-weight call-outs required for export.

Gourmet Asparagus Hang-Tag

Client — Gourmet Trading Company
IndustryProduce / Cold Chain
Year[INSERT YEAR]
RoleGraphic & Label Designer
SoftwareIllustrator, Photoshop

Challenge

Design a compact bi-lingual hang-tag that identifies the grower, grade, and country of origin on a small bundle format, legible at cooler-case distance.

Objectives

Clear brand hierarchy, No.1 grade call-out, bilingual (EN/FR) compliance copy, and a scannable barcode zone — all within a tag no larger than the bundle itself.

Solution

A botanical illustration of asparagus spears anchors the mark; a high-contrast black field carries the grade and compliance information for maximum legibility against the produce backdrop.
